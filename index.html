MISSION

Our mission is to ensure seamless coordination, timely communication, and meticulous record-keeping to facilitate the success of our meetings and major events. We aim to uphold professionalism and excellence in all our endeavors, contributing to the overall success and reputation of the General Management Seminar.

Conference Secretariat and Communications Teams’ Mini-Conference held on February 6, 2024

The Conference Secretariat and Communications teams organized this mini-conference as part of the assignment requirement for the General Management Seminar 2 coursework. The purpose of the event was to provide valuable insights to our fellow students and faculty on the intricacies of event marketing, with a specific focus on advertising an event, using e-marketing, leveraging social media, and designing effective communication tools.

Beach Clean-up held on February 17, 2024

A volunteer initiative spearheaded by our volunteer team, members of the General Management (GM) Seminar 2 visited a section of Palisadoes beach and cleaned up the area. The purpose of the event was to help rid the beach of harmful garbage that affects the environment.

“Color me Wild” held on March 15, 2024

The "Color Me Wild" party was organized by the General Management (GM) Seminar body as a fundraising event, fulfilling a requirement for the General Management Seminar 2 coursework. The purpose of the event was to provide a social gathering where students could come together, build community spirit, and strengthen campus connections.

Major Conference held on March 26, 2024

The major conference was one of the primary goals of the General Management (GM) Seminar. The Seminar body raised funds through organized fundraisers and initiatives to successfully stage this Student Academic Conference. The purpose of the event was to provide a platform for discussing pertinent issues that impact organizational management. The conference delved into topics such as leadership, technology and management, social responsibility, and crisis management. Consequently, students had the opportunity to interact with and gain knowledge from practitioners in various sectors.
